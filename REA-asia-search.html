When I first joined the team, I started working with team members across the countries, manage time differences and meeting times and most importantly, I learnt about each market.


The consumer behaviour in Asia is vastly different. I had to speak to so many people in different journey, from different countries to try and understand their problem, needs and expectations.




Research

We kicked off the project by looking at the pain points from consumer survey sentiments, Google Analytics data, past feedback from focus group studies and customer experience sentiments.


Pain Points
  • Strongest issue came from Hong Kong and their dislike of having to type Traditional Chinese. Users are complaining that other competitor sites allow them to choose and select without typing.
  • The Asia group takes on a regional approach for feature releases. I had to make sure that our solution could work on all market or needed to be tailored slightly.
  • Our audience in Asia wanted to do property search by places of interests.

I came up with a rough mock up and tested it in the market. It went well but there was a major problem with its entry point. After the analysis, I worked with other designers in market to run internal user testing to validate ideas and values. Everything else is a bonus. I conducted 10 sessions for Singapore through VC and had great feedbacks on what's relevant to the country.


Another option that everyone wanted was to live close to public transport facilities. Singapore and Hong Kong have great train systems, Malaysia and Indonesia are again, much bigger geographically and so there are alot of different train systems.

Challenges

First testing sessions were conducted in Indonesia where some participants could only turn up at night (factored in heavy traffic and getting transport). We had a couple of late night interview sessions that really affected our days. In retrospective, next time I'd travel to be closer to the participants around their lunch times.


Because of budget and time constraint, we could only conduct user testings with internal participants. It was difficult for the participants to put themselves in consumers' shoes. Our designers in market are still honing their interview skills and might not yield accurate results.


This is a work in progress. If you want to know more, drop me a line and we can chat over coffee.
