Search Refinements

Category
Mobile Apps
Brand
realcommercial.com.au
Web URL
www.realcommercial.com.au

This is my favourite and the most challenging project out of the overall app uplift. Search filter is the bread and butter of any listing site. We wanted to get it right and it took us 6 months to do plenty of research and refine the UI.

I was embedded in the mobile squad and for this project, I paired up with the designer from the web squad to ensure our work in improving realcommerical.com.au search experience are aligned and consistent.



Research

We kicked off the project by looking at the pain points from our annual consumer survey, Omniture data on filter usage, relevant comments from previous user testing and also from the benchmark test of the current refinements in app.


Consumer Pain Points
  • Difficulty in locating refinements section. Big "Sign in" button in the middle plus the message "No Alerts of Saved Searches" were confusing when the expectation was to do a search.
  • All the input fields were `free text input`, this increased potential error and cognitive load. Most users did not know how much floor space they need or what is the lowest and highest.
  • Users avoid certain fields because they yield "0" result and causes frustration.

So I did more research on each filters. For certain fields, I worked with another designer who had already done the preset values. For keywords, I gathered all the keywords that were usually used and created a list of set values, so users can select them instead of typing it in.


I discovered that users who are looking to buy and rent are using different keywords. So we provide different suggested keywords for users. These keywords are often facilities or property type that we do not provide as options. Why not fix it you say? It would take way too much time and money.

Challenges

I work to create a better consumer experience but I also have to take our customers into considerations. Consumers are our regular site users and customers are the real estate agents. The biggest challenge is in the juggling act of providing value and fulfilling experience for our users but also to not hurt our customers.


Another challenge came from tech constraints and so we had to be creative and work around things. As mentioned above, we used keywords suggestions instead of allowing properties to be tagged with certain keywords, or even fix the property type selections.

UI

Once we have these down, I worked on the UI. I started low-fi but we also wanted to test a variety of input selection methods. From drop down list, full take over list, grid view and slider. Back then everyone loved the airbnb slider, however we found that it was difficult to hit exact amount. The increment is too sensitive. So my assumptions were that, with preset values on the slider, it should not be difficult to choose the desired value (bonus: it has haptic feedback on iOS).


In the business, the iOS apps take priority because there are a lot more iPhone users than Android in Australia (the numbers might be different now). And being in charged of both, I worked on iOS UI first, absorbing the iOS 10 chunky font and rounded corners. It was great to be one of the earliest app in the market with the new native elements.
